About Satopanth Expedition

The Satopanth Expedition is a rare gem among Himalayan adventures, combining the thrill of high-altitude trekking with spiritual significance. The trail leads to Satopanth Tal, a remote glacial lake at 15,100 ft, revered by sages and saints.

This journey traverses dense forests, alpine meadows, moraines, and glacial terrains, offering panoramic views of majestic peaks like Chaukhamba, Neelkanth, and Balakun. It’s a true expedition for those seeking both physical challenge and inner peace.

The trek starts from Mana Village—the last Indian village near the Indo-Tibetan border—and follows the Alaknanda river into untouched wilderness. The legend says that Lord Vishnu bathed in Satopanth Lake and that the holy Trinity (Brahma, Vishnu, Mahesh) meditate at its banks.

With extreme weather and technical terrain, Satopanth is not for the faint-hearted, but those who complete it take home not just a summit badge, but an experience of spiritual connection and Himalayan purity.
