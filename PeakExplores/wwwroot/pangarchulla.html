About Pangarchulla Peak Trek

Pangarchulla Peak, standing tall in the Garhwal Himalayas, is a perfect blend of snow adventure and high-altitude thrill. The summit offers a panoramic 360-degree view of Nanda Devi, Dronagiri, Chaukhamba, and other majestic peaks.

The trail begins from Joshimath and gradually climbs through lush forests, alpine meadows, and snowy ridges. The summit push involves steep sections and snow-clad terrain, making it a rewarding challenge for trekkers looking to transition to technical climbs.

This trek is ideal for experienced trekkers with a good fitness level. You'll experience camping under star-lit skies, walking through forests of oak and rhododendron, and the exhilarating summit climb that will test your grit and offer unmatched Himalayan views.

Itinerary

Drive from Rishikesh to Joshimath. Overnight stay in guest house or hotel.

Drive to Dhak and trek to Gulling Top through forest trails. Campsite with great views.

Easy to moderate trek through meadows and alpine vegetation to Khullara camp.

Rest, explore around, prepare for the summit. Acclimatization is key for success.

Start early, summit Pangarchulla Peak via snowy ridgelines, and return to base.

Descend back to Dhak and drive to Joshimath. Overnight stay in hotel.

Drive back to Rishikesh with memories of the mighty Pangarchulla climb.

⚠️ IMPORTANT NOTE

  • High Altitude Alert: Pangarchulla reaches over 15,000 ft. Proper acclimatization is critical. Avoid alcohol and hydrate regularly.
  • Physical Fitness Required: This trek includes steep climbs and long hiking days. A minimum of 4 weeks of cardio and strength training is recommended.
  • Weather Dependency: The summit day can be affected by snow or wind conditions. Be prepared for itinerary changes for your safety.
  • Gear Essentials: Good quality trekking shoes with ankle support and microspikes for summit day are essential. Carry layered clothing and a windproof jacket.
  • Connectivity: There is no mobile network beyond Joshimath. Inform family and friends in advance.
  • Trekking Etiquette: Maintain silence in forest areas, do not litter, and respect local customs and culture.
  • Leave No Trace: Carry back all non-biodegradable waste. Pangarchulla is a fragile alpine ecosystem.
  • Summit Not Guaranteed: Reaching the summit depends on weather, group fitness, and guide discretion. Safety is the priority.
