About Panpatia Col Trek

The Panpatia Col Trek is one of the most challenging and lesser-explored Himalayan routes. It connects the sacred towns of Badrinath and Kedarnath via the high-altitude Panpatia Glacier and Col.

With steep ascents, moraine crossings, glacier navigation, and unpredictable weather, this trek is for seasoned adventurers looking to test their endurance in a raw Himalayan wilderness.

You'll witness snowfields, pristine meadows, and grand peaks like Neelkanth and Chaukhamba. The myth says that sages once used this path in a single day – today, it takes about 12.

Panpatia Col is not just a trek—it’s an expedition into the heart of ancient Himalayan legends, where spirituality and adventure meet.

Itinerary

Acclimatize and explore the holy town of Badrinath.

Start your trek near India’s last village and hike into alpine pastures.

Ascend through boulder fields and river crossings.

Enter glacial terrain and camp near moraine ridges.

Navigate the vast Panpatia Glacier; roped up with technical gear.

Conquer the high Col with magnificent views of Garhwal peaks.

Steep descent through snowfields to a sacred lake.

Visit the remote temple of Madhmaheshwar en route to Kedarnath.

Exit the high-altitude trail and reach the roadhead.

Relax and celebrate your expedition success.

Reserved for contingencies or relaxation in Rudraprayag.

End of an unforgettable Himalayan experience.
