About the Trek

Har Ki Doon, often referred to as the "Valley of Gods," is a breathtaking cradle-shaped valley nestled in the Garhwal Himalayas of Uttarakhand, India. At an altitude of around 11,700 feet, this valley is rich in mythological and historical significance, believed to be the path taken by the Pandavas on their way to Swargarohini, the mythical stairway to heaven.

The trek to Har Ki Doon offers an immersive journey through one of the most pristine and scenic routes in the Indian Himalayas, making it a favorite among trekkers, nature enthusiasts, and photographers alike.

The trail begins from the charming village of Sankri and weaves through lush green valleys, terraced fields, ancient wooden villages like Taluka, Osla, and Seema, dense deodar and pine forests, and vibrant alpine meadows adorned with seasonal wildflowers. One of the major highlights of the trek is walking alongside the Supin River, with constant views of snow-capped giants such as Swargarohini, Bandarpoonch, Kala Nag (Black Peak), and Jaundhar Glacier.

The valley not only offers majestic views but also provides glimpses into the simple yet culturally rich lifestyle of the local villagers, who still follow age-old customs and architectural traditions. During spring and summer, the valley bursts into color with blooming rhododendrons and orchids, while in autumn, the golden landscapes create a magical ambiance. Although the region is accessible for most of the year, the best time to visit Har Ki Doon is between April to June and September to November, as the trail is closed during the heavy snowfall of peak winter.

The moderate gradient of the trek makes it suitable for beginners with decent fitness, while still offering a rewarding and enriching experience for seasoned trekkers. Whether you're drawn by its mythical stories, its lush and varied terrain, or the tranquility that envelops the valley, Har Ki Doon promises a deeply memorable Himalayan adventure unlike any other.

Itinerary

Drive from Delhi to Sankri via Mussoorie. Overnight stay at Sankri.

Drive to Taluka and trek 12 km to Cheludgad. Overnight stay in tents.

Trek to Har Ki Doon Valley. Enjoy views of Swargarohini and other peaks. Overnight camp.

Acclimatization and exploration day. Visit Marinda Tal or Jaundar Glacier.

Trek back to Cheludgad via same route. Overnight camp stay.

Descend to Taluka and drive to Sankri. Overnight guesthouse stay.

Drive back to Delhi. Trek ends with memories of a lifetime.
