About Roopkund Trek

Roopkund, often referred to as the “Mystery Lake,” is one of India’s most intriguing and sought-after high-altitude treks, located at an elevation of approximately 16,500 feet in the lap of the Himalayas. This glacier lake earned global attention due to the discovery of hundreds of ancient human skeletons that surface when the snow melts—an archaeological enigma that continues to fascinate trekkers and researchers alike.

The journey begins from the quaint village of Lohajung, leading adventurers through an awe-inspiring blend of dense oak and rhododendron forests, crystal-clear streams, and expansive alpine meadows like Ali Bugyal and Bedni Bugyal—two of the most scenic high-altitude grasslands in the country. As you ascend higher, the trail unfolds dramatic ridgelines, high-altitude campsites, and changing landscapes, offering a thrilling mix of adventure and natural beauty.

The trek not only tests one’s endurance but also rewards with stunning panoramic views of the towering Himalayan peaks, including Mt. Trishul, Nanda Ghunti, Chaukhamba, and Neelkanth. The final approach to Roopkund Lake, through a snow-laden, steep glacier climb, is as exhilarating as it is challenging, making it a dream trek for seasoned hikers.

Roopkund Trek is best suited for experienced trekkers who are physically fit and mentally prepared for high-altitude conditions. The best time to undertake this trek is between May to June and September to October, when the trails are accessible and the weather is relatively stable. Whether it’s the haunting mystery of the lake, the surreal landscapes, or the rich cultural encounters in remote Himalayan villages, Roopkund offers a deeply enriching and unforgettable experience.

Itinerary

Drive to Lohajung (7-8 hrs). Scenic mountain roads. Stay in guesthouse.

Trek starts with a gentle trail to Didna village. Stay in tents/homestay.

Steep ascent through forests opens to the stunning Ali Bugyal meadows.

Trek through Bedni Bugyal, camp at Ghora Lotani.

High-altitude climb to Bhagwabasa. Cold and windy conditions.

Early summit attempt. Visit Roopkund lake, return to lower altitude for stay.

Descend to Wan and return by vehicle to Lohajung.

Drive back to Kathgodam and conclude the trek.

⚠️ IMPORTANT NOTE

  • Altitude Acclimatization: Roopkund lies at over 15,700 ft; proper acclimatization is critical. Avoid rapid ascents and stay hydrated throughout the trek.
  • Fitness Requirements: This trek demands strong cardiovascular and muscular endurance. Prepare in advance with stamina training, including running, hiking, and climbing stairs.
  • Medical Caution: Avoid the trek if you suffer from high-altitude sickness, asthma, or any heart-related conditions without consulting your doctor.
  • Connectivity: Mobile signals are limited beyond Wan village. Inform family and friends about your offline status during the trek.
  • Weather Uncertainty: Roopkund weather is unpredictable—snowstorms and rain can affect the itinerary. Be prepared with waterproof gear and warm layers.
  • Forest Permits and Entry Restrictions: Due to Nanda Devi Biosphere restrictions, Roopkund may require special permits or may be seasonally restricted. Stay updated through your trek organizer.
  • Environmental Responsibility: Roopkund is an ecologically sensitive zone. Carry back all your non-biodegradable waste. Avoid using plastic and chemical-based toiletries near water sources.
  • Awareness: Respect the sanctity of Roopkund’s historic human remains. Do not touch or tamper with the skeletal remains found near the lake.
  • Guided Trek Only: Due to trail complexity and remoteness, always trek with a certified local guide or through a registered trekking company.
